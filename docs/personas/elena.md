# Persona: Elena Vasquez — Non-Technical Learner

## Demographics

- **Age:** 52
- **Occupation:** Small business owner (bakery)
- **Location:** San Antonio, Texas, USA
- **Technical comfort:** Low — uses her laptop for email, social media, and basic web browsing; rarely installs desktop software

## Background

Elena wants to learn basic bookkeeping and small business finance. Her niece, a developer, created a course in Course Imports and sent her the app along with instructions. Elena has never heard of GitHub and doesn't know what markdown is. She just wants to open the app, follow the instructions her niece gave her, and learn.

## Goals

- Open the app and load the course her niece shared without needing technical knowledge
- Read through material at a comfortable pace
- Feel confident she understands each topic before moving on (quizzes help)
- Not accidentally lose her place or progress

## Typical workflows

1. Opens Course Imports from her Applications folder
2. Pastes the URL her niece texted her into the input field
3. Clicks "Load course" and waits
4. Reads topics sequentially, one at a time, using the sidebar to navigate
5. Answers quiz questions and reads the explanations carefully
6. Closes the app when she's done for the day
7. Reopens later and clicks the course from Recent Courses to continue

## Pain points and frustrations

- Any technical jargon in error messages will stop her in her tracks — she'll close the app and text her niece
- Doesn't understand what "GitHub personal access token" means and would be alarmed by the settings panel asking for one
- If the app looks broken or "developer-y" she'll assume she's doing something wrong
- Needs very clear visual cues for what to click and what's happening (loading states, progress, completion)

## What success looks like

Elena pastes the URL, the course loads without issues, she works through three topics in her first session, closes the app, and reopens it the next day to find everything exactly as she left it. She never needs to ask her niece for help.
