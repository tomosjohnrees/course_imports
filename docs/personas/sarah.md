# Persona: Sarah Chen — Self-Directed Learner

## Demographics

- **Age:** 28
- **Occupation:** Marketing coordinator
- **Location:** Melbourne, Australia
- **Technical comfort:** Moderate — uses apps daily, comfortable with browsers and basic software, but doesn't code or use the terminal

## Background

Sarah has always been curious. She picks up new interests constantly — last month it was watercolour painting, this month it's introductory data analysis. She prefers structured, self-paced learning over videos or unstructured tutorials. She tried several online platforms but found them either too expensive for casual exploration or too shallow. A friend shared a GitHub link to a course and told her to paste it into Course Imports.

## Goals

- Learn new topics at her own pace without subscription pressure
- Have a clear sense of progress ("how far through am I?")
- Pick up where she left off between sessions
- Try a wide variety of subjects without committing to a platform

## Typical workflows

1. Receives a GitHub course URL from a friend, Reddit post, or blog
2. Pastes the URL into the home screen and loads the course
3. Works through 2–3 topics per sitting, usually in the evening
4. Uses the sidebar progress bar to gauge how far she's gotten
5. Returns days later and resumes from where she left off via Recent Courses
6. Answers quizzes to check understanding — appreciates the instant feedback

## Pain points and frustrations

- Gets confused if error messages are too technical (e.g., "API rate limit exceeded")
- Doesn't know what a GitHub personal access token is and wouldn't know where to get one
- Finds it annoying if loading takes a long time with no indication of what's happening
- Wants to feel confident she hasn't lost progress when she closes and reopens the app

## What success looks like

Sarah loads a course in under 10 seconds, works through several topics with clear progress feedback, closes the app, and picks up exactly where she left off the next day — all without needing to understand GitHub, APIs, or file structures.
